Do not add new systems like employees, offices, publishers, burnout, save/load, contracts, or more market mechanics.

This pass is focused only on making the release moment more engaging.

Problem:
The current release report is a tall scrollable modal that shows reviews, revenue, fans, diagnosis, and trend impact all at once. It works functionally, but it does not feel exciting. It feels like a static dashboard.

Goal:
Change the release flow into a staged game-like sequence:

1. Review reveal phase
2. Post-review reaction phase
3. Week-by-week sales phase
4. Final sales summary
5. Next-game motivation

The player should experience the release like an event, not like reading a report.

1. Split release into phases

Add releaseFlow state:

const releaseFlow = {
active: false,
phase: "reviews" | "reaction" | "sales" | "summary",
game: null,
currentReviewIndex: 0,
weeklySalesIndex: 0,
salesWeeks: [],
revealedReviews: []
};

When player clicks “Release Game”:

* Do not instantly show final revenue.
* Open the Review Phase first.
* Blur/dim the garage behind the modal.
* Keep the modal compact and centered.
* No scrollable long report.

2. Review reveal phase

Show one review card at a time.

Sequence:

* Show outlet name first.
* After short delay, reveal quote.
* Then reveal score with a punchy animation.
* Player can click “Next Review” or press Space/Enter.
* After 4 reviews, show average score.

Example outlets:

* Byte Magazine
* Arcade Gazette
* PC Gamer UK
* Digital Frontiers

Each review card:

* Outlet logo/icon placeholder
* Outlet name
* Quote
* Score
* Mood styling:

  * bad: red
  * average: amber
  * good: green
  * excellent: blue/purple

Do not show revenue yet during reviews.

3. Add emotional reaction after reviews

After average score is revealed:
Show a compact reaction screen for 2–3 seconds or until player clicks continue.

Examples:

* Score < 5:
  “Critics were not impressed.”
  Character looks disappointed.
* Score 5–7:
  “A decent first step.”
  Character looks thoughtful.
* Score 7–8.5:
  “The game is getting attention.”
  Character celebrates.
* Score > 8.5:
  “Breakout hit!”
  Strong celebration animation.

Update the developer character in the garage behind the modal:

* bad score: slumped
* average: neutral
* good: celebrating
* excellent: jumping/strong celebration

4. Sales phase should happen week by week

After reviews, begin sales simulation.

Do not show total revenue immediately.

Show:

* Week 1 sales
* Week 2 sales
* Week 3 sales
* etc.

Each sales week should reveal sequentially:

* Units sold this week
* Revenue this week
* Fans gained this week
* Running total revenue
* Running total fans

Use a simple animated sales ticker:
“Week 1: 138 units sold · $1,840 revenue · +22 fans”

Each week should feel like a small event.

Add buttons:

* “Next Week”
* “Auto-play Sales”
* “Skip to Summary”

Auto-play should reveal one week every 1–1.5 seconds.

5. Sales curve

Generate salesWeeks before the sales phase.

Sales should depend on:

* average review score
* fans
* platform sales multiplier
* market trend multiplier
* hype
* bugs penalty
* random variance

Sales curve:

* Week 1 is strongest
* Week 2 lower
* Week 3 lower
* Continue for 4–8 weeks depending on score

Example:

* bad score: 3–4 week tail
* average score: 4–5 week tail
* good score: 6–7 week tail
* excellent score: 8 week tail

Revenue should be added to player money week by week, not all at once.

Fans should also be added week by week.

6. Visual sales feedback in garage

During sales phase, show temporary garage feedback:

* money bubbles near top-left cash card
* fan bubbles near character
* small newspaper/game box icon near desk
* if sales are strong, character celebrates
* if sales are weak, character looks worried

Do not create a full dashboard.

7. Final summary phase

After sales weeks finish, show a compact final summary:

* Game title
* Average review score
* Total units sold
* Total revenue
* Total fans gained
* Profit/loss
* Best score comparison
* Best revenue comparison
* Next target

Also show:

* “New best score!” if applicable
* “Revenue record!” if applicable
* “Fewer bugs than last game!” if applicable
* “Matched market trend!” if applicable

Keep “Why this score?” as a collapsible section or compact panel in this summary phase only.
Do not show diagnosis during the review reveal phase.

8. Improve pacing

The release flow should take about:

* 20–30 seconds if player clicks through normally
* 8–12 seconds if player skips/auto-plays
* never force the player to wait too long

Add keyboard controls:

* Space/Enter: continue
* Escape: skip to summary only after reviews are revealed

9. Remove scroll-heavy modal

The release modal should not require vertical scrolling on desktop.

Use phase-based screens instead:

* reviews screen
* sales screen
* summary screen

Each screen should fit within the viewport.

On smaller screens:

* allow vertical scroll only if absolutely necessary
* keep primary action button sticky at bottom of modal

10. Analytics

Track:

* release_flow_started
* review_revealed
* all_reviews_revealed
* review_average_revealed
* sales_phase_started
* sales_week_revealed
* sales_autoplay_started
* sales_skipped
* final_summary_viewed
* release_flow_completed

Update printSessionSummary() with:

* average time spent in review phase
* did player skip sales
* did player auto-play sales
* total sales weeks viewed
* did player reach final summary

11. Preserve existing systems

Do not break:

* existing scoring formula
* diagnosis panel
* trend bonus
* combo memory
* upgrade bonuses
* next target
* bubbles
* analytics
* objective pill
* project pill

This is a presentation/pacing refactor, not a mechanics rewrite.

12. Deliverable

Return the complete updated HTML file with embedded CSS and JS.
It must run directly in browser.
No external dependencies.
No placeholder TODOs.
No “next I can add...” summary instead of code.

Success criteria:
Releasing a game should feel like a mini-event:

* first the player waits for reviews
* then sees emotional reaction
* then watches sales week by week
* then gets a compact final summary
* then understands what to improve in the next game
