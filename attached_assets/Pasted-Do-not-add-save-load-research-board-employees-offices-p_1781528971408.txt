Do not add save/load, research board, employees, offices, publishers, new contracts, custom engines, or more strategy systems.

This pass is focused only on making the release/review/sales moment feel like a real tycoon-game payoff.

Current issue:
The current release flow is still too modal-driven and click-driven. The player should not have to click “Next Review” or “Next Week” repeatedly to experience the release. Reviews and sales should unfold automatically in real time with animations.

Goal:
Make the release moment feel closer to classic game-dev tycoon pacing:

* review scores reveal automatically
* each review score rolls through numbers before landing on its final score
* sales happen week by week in real time
* sales cards show animated mini charts
* revenue/fans update live
* player can watch the game sell without clicking every week

Important:
Do not copy Game Dev Tycoon UI, assets, exact layout, icons, names, or copyrighted identity. Create an original system with similar pacing and emotional structure.

1. Replace click-to-reveal reviews with automatic review reveal

When player releases a game:

* Start the review reveal sequence automatically.
* Do not require clicking “Next Review.”
* Each review outlet appears one at a time.
* Each score animates before settling on its predetermined final score.

Example visual behavior:

* final score is 10
* score display rapidly cycles:
  7 → 9 → 8 → 10 → 9 → 10
* then lands on:
  10!

Rules:

* The final score must be calculated before animation starts.
* The animation must never change the real score.
* Intermediate numbers are only visual suspense.
* Final average must use the real predetermined review scores, not the animated intermediate values.

2. Add review score roll animation

Create a reusable function/component:

animateReviewScore({
finalScore,
durationMs,
onUpdate,
onComplete
})

Behavior:

* For 800–1400ms, display rapidly changing numbers near the final score.
* Numbers should generally hover around the final score.
* Bad game: mostly low numbers.
* Great game: mostly high numbers.
* Excellent game: allow dramatic near-10 flashes.
* End exactly on finalScore.
* Add a short punch animation when final score lands.

Examples:

* final 4.5 → 3, 5, 4, 6, 4.5
* final 7.2 → 6, 8, 7, 7.5, 7.2
* final 10 → 8, 9, 10, 9.5, 10!

3. Automatic review sequence timing

Review phase should run without user input:

Timeline:

* 0.0s: outlet card appears
* 0.5s: quote appears
* 0.8s: score roll starts
* 2.0s: final score lands
* 2.5s: next outlet begins

After 4 reviews:

* show average score reveal
* average score also rolls briefly before landing
* then transition automatically to sales phase after 2–3 seconds

Controls:

* Add small “Skip Reviews” button
* Add small “Fast Forward” button
* Space/Enter can speed up current reveal, but clicking should not be required

4. Make review screen more game-like

The review screen should not be a tall scrollable modal.

Use a compact centered overlay:

* game title
* topic / genre / platform
* current outlet card
* four small slots showing review progress
* average score area appears only after all reviews

Each revealed review slot should show:

* outlet icon

* color mood

Do not show revenue, fans, diagnosis, or sales during review phase.

5. Add real-time sales phase

After reviews finish, start sales automatically.

The player should not need to click “Next Week.”

Create sales simulation state:

releaseFlow.sales = {
active: true,
weekIndex: 0,
weeks: [],
runningUnits: 0,
runningRevenue: 0,
runningFans: 0,
autoplay: true,
completed: false
}

Sales weeks are generated before the phase starts, but revealed one by one automatically.

Each sales week reveals every 1.0–1.5 seconds:

* Week number
* Units sold this week
* Revenue this week
* Fans gained this week
* Running total

Player controls:

* Pause Sales
* Resume Sales
* Skip to Summary
* Speed x1 / x2

Default should be autoplay.

6. Add animated sales cards with mini charts

Add 1–2 compact sales cards, similar in purpose to tycoon sales widgets, but original in design.

Each sales card should show:

* game title
* total units sold
* small animated bar chart
* current week revenue
* running total revenue
* fans gained

Chart behavior:

* one bar per sales week
* each week adds a new bar
* bar height based on units sold that week
* Week 1 usually highest
* later weeks decay, unless review score/hype/fans are strong

Do not use a full dashboard.
These should feel like floating sales widgets or report cards over the garage.

7. Sales formula

Sales should depend on:

* average review score
* existing fans before release
* platform sales multiplier
* hype
* trend multiplier
* bug penalty
* random variance

Suggested model:

* baseDemand = 80 + fans * 0.08 + hype * 12
* reviewMultiplier:

  * score < 5: 0.4–0.8
  * score 5–7: 0.8–1.2
  * score 7–8.5: 1.2–1.8
  * score > 8.5: 1.8–3.0
* week decay:

  * Week 1 = 100%
  * Week 2 = 70–85%
  * Week 3 = 50–70%
  * later weeks decay
* excellent games can have longer tails
* bad games die quickly

Tail length:

* bad score: 3–4 weeks
* average score: 4–5 weeks
* good score: 6–7 weeks
* excellent score: 8–10 weeks

Revenue and fans must be added week by week, not all upfront.
